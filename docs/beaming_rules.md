# Beaming Rules Reference

The following rules dictate how consecutive notes (quavers and semiquavers) should be beamed together based on Grade 1 Music Theory.

## General Rules
1. **Purpose:** Quavers and semiquavers should be joined to make the crotchet beat obvious.
2. **Bar Lines:** Beams never cross over bar lines.
3. **Off Beats:** The first note of a beamed group must never fall on an off beat, unless preceded by a rest or a dotted note.
4. **Mixed Stem Directions:** If a group contains notes that would individually have stems pointing in different directions, determine the master direction by finding the note furthest from the middle line of the staff. Use the natural stem direction of that furthest note for the entire group.
5. **Rests:** Rests can be included inside a group of beamed notes. The beam simply passes over the rest (the rest itself is not beamed). 

## Time Signature Specifics (Assuming Crotchet Beats)

### 2/4 Time (2 Crotchet beats)
- Notes are normally beamed to make up one crotchet beat.
- Exception: If there are four quavers in a bar, they can all be beamed together across the entire bar.

### 3/4 Time (3 Crotchet beats)
- Quavers can be beamed right across two or three whole crotchet beats.
- The first note of the group must fall on the beat, not an off beat.
- Groups containing semiquavers are normally only beamed to make up a maximum of one crotchet beat.

### 4/4 Time (4 Crotchet beats)
- The bar has a strong beat (1), weak beat (2), secondary strong beat (3), and weak beat (4).
- Quavers can be beamed to make up to two crotchets' worth of beats if they fall on beats 1-2 or 3-4.
- **NEVER** beam across beats 2 and 3 (the middle of the bar), as this hides the secondary strong beat.
- Groups containing semiquavers should normally equal a maximum of one or two crotchets (respecting the middle-bar break).
